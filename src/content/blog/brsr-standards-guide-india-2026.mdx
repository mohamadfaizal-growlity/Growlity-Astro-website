## **What Are BRSR Standards?**

Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting ([BRSR](https://brsr.in/)) is a disclosure framework introduced by the Securities and Exchange Board of India for listed companies in India. The framework helps businesses disclose their Environmental, Social, and Governance ([ESG](https://growlity.com/solutions/esg-assessment)) performance in a structured and measurable manner.

BRSR replaced the earlier Business Responsibility Report (BRR) to create more standardized, transparent, and data-driven sustainability disclosures.

The objective of BRSR is simple:

*   Improve ESG transparency
*   Encourage responsible business conduct
*   Align Indian reporting with global sustainability expectations
*   Help investors make informed decisions
*   Promote long-term sustainable growth

Today, BRSR is becoming a critical part of corporate strategy rather than just a compliance exercise.

## **Understanding the Structure of BRSR Reporting**

The BRSR framework is divided into multiple sections that collectively evaluate a company’s sustainability performance.

### **Section A: General Disclosures**

This section covers:

*   Company profile
*   Operations
*   Employee data
*   Product and service details
*   CSR initiatives
*   Market presence

### **Section B: Management and Process Disclosures**

This focuses on governance structures and sustainability management processes, including:

*   ESG policies
*   Leadership accountability
*   Stakeholder engagement
*   Risk management systems

### **Section C: Principle-Wise Performance Disclosures**

This section evaluates performance against the 9 National Guidelines on Responsible Business Conduct (NGRBC) principles.

These principles cover areas such as:

*   Ethics and transparency
*   Employee well-being
*   Environmental sustainability
*   Human rights
*   Consumer value
*   Inclusive growth

## **The 9 Core Principles Behind BRSR Standards**

Understanding the principles behind the framework is essential for effective implementation.

### **Principle 1: Ethics, Transparency, and Accountability**

Businesses should operate ethically and maintain transparent governance practices.

### **Principle 2: Sustainable Products and Services**

Companies must design products and services responsibly throughout their lifecycle.

### **Principle 3: Employee Well-Being**

Organizations should prioritize fair treatment, safety, diversity, and employee development.

### **Principle 4: Stakeholder Responsiveness**

Businesses should engage responsibly with all stakeholders.

### **Principle 5: Human Rights**

Companies are expected to protect and respect human rights across operations and supply chains.

### **Principle 6: Environmental Protection**

Organizations must reduce environmental impact and improve sustainability performance.

### **Principle 7: Responsible Public Policy Engagement**

Businesses should engage in policy advocacy responsibly and ethically.

### **Principle 8: Inclusive Growth**

Companies should contribute positively to communities and economic development.

### **Principle 9: Consumer Value**

Organizations should provide safe, transparent, and sustainable value to customers.

### **Who Needs to Comply with BRSR?**

Currently, the top listed entities by market capitalization in India are required to file BRSR reports under SEBI regulations.

However, the influence of BRSR extends beyond listed companies.

Businesses indirectly affected include:

*   MSMEs supplying to large corporations
*   Manufacturers exporting internationally
*   Vendors within ESG-driven supply chains
*   Companies seeking investment or funding
*   Organizations preparing for future ESG regulations

This is why even non-mandated companies are beginning to adopt sustainability reporting frameworks proactively.

FAQs

### What are BRSR Standards?

BRSR Standards, or Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting Standards, are ESG disclosure guidelines introduced by the Securities and Exchange Board of India for listed companies in India. These standards help businesses report their environmental, social, and governance performance in a structured and transparent way. The framework is designed to improve accountability, strengthen investor confidence, and align Indian businesses with global sustainability expectations. In 2026, BRSR Standards are becoming a major benchmark for responsible corporate governance and sustainable business operations.

### Who is required to comply with BRSR reporting in India?

Currently, BRSR reporting is mandatory for the top listed companies in India based on market capitalization under SEBI regulations. However, many non-listed companies, MSMEs, exporters, and supply chain partners are also voluntarily adopting BRSR practices because large corporations and global buyers increasingly expect ESG transparency across the value chain. Businesses preparing early for sustainability reporting are often better positioned for future compliance requirements and investor expectations.

### Why are BRSR Standards important for businesses in 2026?

BRSR Standards are becoming increasingly important because sustainability performance now directly influences investment decisions, supply chain partnerships, brand reputation, and regulatory compliance. Investors are evaluating ESG risks more closely, and global customers prefer businesses that demonstrate responsible operations. In 2026, companies that effectively implement sustainability reporting frameworks are gaining a stronger competitive advantage, better stakeholder trust, and improved long-term resilience.

### What are the key principles covered under BRSR?

BRSR reporting is based on the 9 principles of the National Guidelines on Responsible Business Conduct (NGRBC). These principles focus on ethical governance, employee well-being, environmental protection, human rights, stakeholder engagement, sustainable products and services, inclusive growth, and responsible business practices. Together, these principles help organizations build a more transparent and sustainable operational framework.

### How does BRSR reporting benefit investors and stakeholders?

BRSR reporting gives investors and stakeholders greater visibility into how a company manages sustainability risks and opportunities. Transparent ESG disclosures help stakeholders assess long-term business stability, governance quality, environmental impact, and social responsibility. This increased transparency often improves investor confidence, strengthens stakeholder relationships, and enhances corporate reputation in competitive markets.

### Is BRSR aligned with global ESG frameworks?

Yes, BRSR Standards are designed to align closely with international sustainability reporting trends and ESG expectations. The framework incorporates globally recognized sustainability concepts while adapting them to the Indian business environment. This alignment helps Indian companies improve comparability with international ESG reporting standards and strengthens their position in global markets and supply chains.

### Can BRSR become mandatory for more companies in the future?

The scope of sustainability reporting in India is expected to expand gradually as ESG regulations continue evolving. Many industry experts believe that BRSR-related disclosure expectations could eventually extend beyond the largest listed companies. Businesses that proactively adopt ESG reporting frameworks today are likely to be better prepared for future compliance requirements and market expectations.
