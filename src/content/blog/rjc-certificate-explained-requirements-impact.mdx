## **What Is RJC Certificate**

RJC Certificate (COP 24) is the 2026 global standard for ethical jewellery. It requires strong human rights due diligence, third-party verification of GHG emission, and OECD-aligned sourcing. The process typically takes **6–12 months** and is mandatory for all RJC members as of 2026.

RJC Certificate is a third-party verification process that a member company complies with the Code of Practices across its operations and supply chain activities.

However, in practical terms, certification evaluates whether your company has:

*   A structured management system
*   Formal risk assessment processes
*   Documented supply chain due diligence
*   Operational controls aligned with COP clauses
*   Evidence-based implementation

This is not a policy-checking exercise. It is a systems audit.

Certification applies to manufacturers, refiners, traders, retailers and service providers across the gold, silver, platinum group metals and diamond supply chain.

## **Understanding COP 24: What Actually Changed**

Under COP 24, the focus has shifted from policy existence to operational effectiveness.

Major technical enhancements include:

### **1\. Strengthened Human Rights Due Diligence**

Companies must conduct formal risk assessments aligned with frameworks referenced by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development and the United Nations Guiding Principles.

### **2\. Expanded Supply Chain Traceability**

Supplier onboarding now requires risk classification and documented screening procedures.

### **3\. Enhanced Environmental Monitoring**

Energy consumption, emissions and waste must be tracked systematically, not informally

### **4\. Grievance Mechanism Effectiveness**

Mechanisms must be accessible, documented and demonstrably functional.

### **5\. Diversity: DEI & Gender Equality**

New requirements for formal policies and measurable actions regarding Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) in the workplace.”

### **6\. Climate: [Climate Change](https://growlity.com/solutions/ghg-emissions-inventory-accounting) & Carbon**

Transition from simple energy tracking to mandatory **Scope 1 and Scope 2 Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emission reporting** for larger entities.”

## **RJC Certificate Requirements: Clause-Level Breakdown**

To achieve Responsible Jewellery Council Certification, companies must implement controls across the following domains:

### **Governance & Ethics**

*   Anti-bribery and anti-corruption policies
*   Know Your Counterparty procedures
*   Documented compliance review process

### **Human Rights & Labour**

*   Forced labour risk screening
*   Child labour prevention controls
*   Non-discrimination implementation records
*   Worker grievance logs

### **Health & Safety**

*   Risk assessments
*   Incident reporting registers
*   Training documentation
*   Emergency drills evidence

### **Environmental Management**

*   Waste tracking logs
*   Hazardous material handling records
*   Quantified GHG emission reports (Scope 1 & 2) and documented science-based reduction targets.
*   Environmental risk assessment

### **Responsible Sourcing**

*   Supplier due diligence documentation
*   High-risk supplier mitigation plans
*   Transaction-level screening (where applicable)
*   Proof of **OECD Step 5 reporting** (Public [Annual Sustainability Report](https://growlity.com/solutions/annual-sustainability-report-asr)) is now a critical requirement for high-revenue members.

## **RJC Certificate Audit Process: What Actually Happens**

The RJC Certificate audit is conducted by an accredited third-party auditor.

The process typically includes:

### **Stage 1: Document Review**

The auditor evaluates policies, procedures and risk assessments.

### **Stage 2: On-Site Verification**

*   Management interviews
*   Worker interviews
*   Facility inspection
*   Documentation sampling

### **Stage 3: Non-Conformity Classification**

Findings are classified as:

*   Minor
*   Major
*   Critical

Corrective actions must be implemented within specified timelines.

Companies that treat certification as documentation-only preparation often face avoidable non-conformities.

FAQs

### What is RJC Certificate?

RJC Certificate is a globally recognized certification that verifies whether a company follows responsible and ethical practices across the jewelry and watch supply chain. It is issued by the Responsible Jewellery Council, an international standards organization that promotes responsible business practices in the jewelry industry. RJC certificate confirms that a company complies with strict standards related to ethical sourcing, environmental management, labor rights, human rights, and supply chain transparency. To obtain RJC certificate, companies must undergo an independent third-party audit to demonstrate compliance with the Responsible Jewellery Council’s standards such as the Code of Practices (COP) or Chain of Custody (CoC).

### What is the full form of RJC in RJC Certificate?

The full form of RJC in RJC Certificate is Responsible Jewellery Council. The Responsible Jewellery Council is a global non-profit organization that establishes standards and certification programs for responsible business practices in the jewelry and watch industry. It works with companies across the entire supply chain, including mining, refining, manufacturing, trading, and retail, to ensure that precious metals and gemstones are sourced and processed responsibly.

### What does RJC stand for?

RJC stands for Responsible Jewellery Council, an international organization that sets standards for ethical, social, and environmental responsibility in the jewelry supply chain. Through its certification programs, the Responsible Jewellery Council helps companies demonstrate their commitment to responsible sourcing, business ethics, human rights protection, and environmental sustainability. Businesses that achieve RJC certification show that they meet internationally recognized standards for responsible jewelry production and supply chain management.

### What does RJC stand for in the jewelry industry?

In the jewelry industry, RJC stands for Responsible Jewellery Council, the leading global standards body focused on responsible sourcing and ethical practices in the jewelry and watch sector. Companies that obtain certification from the Responsible Jewellery Council demonstrate that their operations meet internationally recognized requirements related to sustainability, human rights, and ethical supply chain management. As a result, RJC certification is widely considered a benchmark for responsible business practices within the global jewelry industry.
