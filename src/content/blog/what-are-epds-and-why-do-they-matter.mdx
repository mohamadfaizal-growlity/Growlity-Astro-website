Sustainability is becoming a real priority for businesses and consumers alike. But how can we truly know the environmental impact of a product? That’s where Environmental Product Declarations, or EPDs, come in. Think of them as a product’s “report card” for its impact on the planet, based on verified data. In this first part of our series, we’ll break down what EPDs are, why they matter, and how they can help companies make smarter, greener decisions.

## **What Is an Environmental Product Declaration (EPD)?**

In today’s world, companies are under pressure to prove that their products are not just high quality, but also environmentally responsible. This is where an Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) comes in. An EPD is more than just paperwork, it’s a scientifically backed report that explains a product’s environmental footprint in a transparent and standardized way. By [publishing](https://www.environdec.com/home) an EPD, businesses give their customers, regulators, and investors reliable data about their product’s sustainability performance.

## **Simple Definition**

An Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) is a third-party verified document that shows the environmental impact of a product across its lifecycle. It covers aspects like carbon emissions, energy use, water consumption, and waste generation. In simple terms, you can think of it as a “nutrition label for the environment,” helping stakeholders compare products based on facts, not marketing claims.

## **Standards Behind EPDs**

EPDs follow strict international standards to ensure credibility. The backbone is ISO 14025, which governs Type III environmental declarations. To prepare an [EPD](https://growlity.com/solution/environmental-product-declaration-epd), a Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) is carried out, mapping every stage of the product’s journey, from raw materials to end-of-life. Once the data is collected, an independent third party verifies it, making sure the EPD is accurate and trustworthy.

## **Cradle-to-Gate vs Cradle-to-Grave**

Not all EPDs cover the same scope. Some focus only on the manufacturing phase (cradle-to-gate), while others look at the entire product lifecycle, including use and disposal (cradle-to-grave). The more comprehensive the scope, the more useful the EPD becomes for sustainability reporting, procurement, and ESG disclosures.

## **Why Do EPDs Matter Today?**

Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs) have evolved from being a specialized compliance tool to a strategic asset for businesses. Today, companies are under increasing pressure from regulators, investors, and customers to demonstrate the environmental footprint of their products. EPDs provide a clear, verified, and internationally recognized way to measure and communicate a product’s environmental impact across its life cycle. By using EPDs, companies can make informed decisions about sustainability, reduce risk, and create value in competitive markets.

## **From Compliance to Strategy**

Initially, EPDs were mainly used to meet regulatory requirements or standards, especially in industries like construction or manufacturing. Over time, they have become a strategic tool that can guide business decisions, product innovation, and supply chain optimization. Companies that adopt EPDs early can differentiate themselves in the market, showing that sustainability is part of their core business strategy. By integrating EPD data into product development and marketing, businesses can gain a competitive edge while promoting transparency and responsibility.

## **Building Trust and Transparency**

EPDs serve as a credible, third-party verified source of information about a product’s environmental impact. They help build trust with customers, regulators, and investors by providing transparent and scientifically backed data. For mature businesses, this transparency is critical. It not only improves brand reputation but also strengthens relationships across the supply chain, as partners increasingly demand proof of sustainability performance before collaboration.

## **Supporting Net Zero and Scope 3 Reporting**

As global attention shifts to climate action, EPDs play a key role in supporting net zero strategies. They provide reliable data for calculating Scope 3 emissions, which cover indirect emissions across a product’s life cycle. **Despite their importance, only about 19% of existing EPDs currently include Scope 3 information.** For companies serious about sustainability reporting and achieving net zero targets, EPDs offer a measurable and standardized approach to track environmental performance and reduce emissions throughout the supply chain.

## ****Conclusion****

EPDs are more than just technical paperwork, they’re a starting point for real transparency and smarter sustainability choices. By knowing what they are and how they work, businesses can build trust and get ready for a future that values low-carbon products. FAQs

## On Environmental Product Declarations

We’ve answered the most common questions to help you navigate the world of EPDs with confidence.

### What exactly is an EPD?

An Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) is a verified document that reports a product’s environmental impact throughout its lifecycle, from production to disposal.

### Why are EPDs important for companies?

EPDs provide transparent data that helps businesses make sustainable choices, improve processes, and show customers their commitment to the environment.

### Do EPDs apply to all types of products?

Yes, EPDs can be created for almost any product or material, though they are most common in construction, manufacturing, and consumer goods.

### Who verifies the information in an EPD?

A third-party certification body checks the data to ensure it is accurate, consistent, and follows international standards like ISO 14025.

### Can EPDs help consumers make better choices?

Absolutely! EPDs allow customers to compare products based on verified environmental impact rather than marketing claims.
