# **What is EcoVadis? A Complete Guide for Businesses**

[EcoVadis](https://growlity.com/solutions/ecovadis-assessment) is the world’s most trusted provider of business sustainability ratings, evaluating over 130,000 companies across 200+ industries. It provides an evidence-based platform that assesses ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) performance to help businesses manage supply chain risk and improve global impact. 

[EcoVadis](https://ecovadis.com/) is a global ESG rating platform that evaluates how effectively companies **implement sustainability practices across their operations and supply chains**. It provides a standardized score (0–100) used by global buyers to assess supplier risk and compliance. 

In today’s global supply chains, sustainability is no longer optional—it is a business requirement. One of the most widely used platforms to evaluate corporate sustainability performance is EcoVadis.

This guide explains what EcoVadis is, how the assessment works, and why your score can directly impact revenue, supplier relationships, and market access. Whether you are new to EcoVadis or looking to improve your performance, this blog is designed to be your **go-to resource**.

## **What is EcoVadis and Why Does It Matter?**

EcoVadis is a global sustainability rating platform that evaluates companies across environmental, social, and ethical criteria. Businesses across industries use assessment to assess suppliers, manage [ESG risks](https://growlity.com/solutions/climate-risk-assessment), and improve transparency.

The EcoVadis framework is built on international standards such as:

*   [GRI](https://www.globalreporting.org/) (Global Reporting Initiative)
*   [UN Global Compact](https://unglobalcompact.org/)
*   [ISO 26000](https://www.iso.org/iso-26000-social-responsibility.html)

For businesses, it is not just a rating it is a **commercial enabler**. Many global companies now require suppliers to maintain a minimum score to remain in their value chain.

In practice, most companies don’t lose points due to poor sustainability performance they lose points due to **lack of documentation and structured evidence**. Companies that align policies, actions, and reporting typically see faster score improvements.

## **How the EcoVadis Assessment Works**

The EcoVadis assessment evaluates companies across four key pillars:

### **1\. Environment**

Covers emissions, energy use, waste management, and climate strategy.

### **2\. Labor & Human Rights**

Focuses on employee welfare, diversity, and working conditions.

### **3\. Ethics**

Includes anti-corruption, business integrity, and compliance systems.

### **4\. Sustainable Procurement**

Assesses how companies manage supplier sustainability.

Each company completes a detailed questionnaire and submits supporting documents. EcoVadis analysts then validate the information and assign a score.

## **Understanding the EcoVadis Scorecard**

The score ranges from 0 to 100. As of 2026, medals are awarded based on your **Percentile Rank** compared to all companies in the database.

**Important:** To qualify for a medal, a company must achieve a minimum score of **30** in each of the four themes (Environment, Labor & Human Rights, Ethics, and Sustainable Procurement).

## **How EcoVadis Weights Your Submission**

It doesn’t just look at what you _say_; they look at what you _do_. Their analysts weigh your score based on three layers:

1.  **Policies (25%):** Your formal commitments and management goals.
2.  **Actions (40%):** Implementation of the policies (e.g., training, equipment, procedures).
3.  **Results (35%):** KPIs, reporting, and 360° Watch findings.

## **Common Challenges Companies Face in EcoVadis**

Despite its benefits, many companies struggle with good score due to:

*   Submitting policies without proof of implementation
*   Missing KPIs or measurable results
*   Uploading outdated or non-relevant documents
*   Ignoring [supplier sustainability](https://growlity.com/solutions/value-chain-partner-assessment) practices
*   Not aligning with frameworks like Global Reporting Initiative

These challenges often lead to low scores even when companies are performing well operationally.

## **The EcoVadis Carbon Action Module (CAM)**

As global regulations like the **EU’s CSRD** and **CSDDD** take effect, EcoVadis has introduced the Carbon Action Module. This is a separate scorecard specifically for tracking [Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions](https://growlity.com/solutions/ghg-emissions-inventory-accounting).

To achieve high scores in 2026, companies must demonstrate:

*   **Carbon Footprint Measurement:** Reporting Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions.
*   **Target Setting:** Aligning with [Science Based Targets (SBTi)](https://growlity.com/solutions/science-based-targets-initiative-sbti).
*   **Supplier Engagement:** How you are helping your own suppliers decarbonize.

FAQs

### What is EcoVadis?

EcoVadis is a globally recognized sustainability rating platform that evaluates companies based on their environmental, social, and ethical performance. It provides a standardized methodology to assess how well organizations integrate sustainability into their operations and supply chains. The assessment is evidence-based, meaning companies must submit documented proof of policies, actions, and results. This makes it not just a theoretical framework, but a practical tool used by multinational corporations to evaluate and monitor supplier sustainability performance. As global supply chains become more transparent, it is increasingly considered a benchmark for responsible business practices.

### What are the 4 pillars of EcoVadis?

The framework is structured around four key pillars: Environment, Labor & Human Rights, Ethics, and Sustainable Procurement. Each pillar represents a critical dimension of corporate sustainability and is evaluated based on both policy commitments and implementation evidence. The Environment pillar focuses on areas such as carbon emissions, energy use, and waste management, while Labor & Human Rights examines employee welfare, diversity, and workplace conditions. The Ethics pillar assesses anti-corruption measures and business integrity, and Sustainable Procurement evaluates how companies manage ESG risks within their supply chains. Together, these pillars provide a comprehensive and balanced view of a company’s sustainability performance under the methodology.

### Is EcoVadis worth it?

EcoVadis is increasingly becoming a business-critical requirement rather than an optional sustainability initiative. For companies operating in global supply chains, a strong EcoVadis score can directly impact client retention, supplier onboarding, and access to new markets. Organizations that invest in improving their EcoVadis performance often see benefits such as enhanced credibility, reduced ESG risks, and stronger alignment with international standards. While the assessment process requires time and resources, the long-term value it delivers in terms of competitive advantage and stakeholder trust makes EcoVadis a worthwhile investment for most businesses aiming to scale sustainably.

### How is the EcoVadis score calculated?

The score is calculated based on a comprehensive evaluation of a company’s policies, actions, and results across the four sustainability pillars. Each section is weighted according to the company’s industry, size, and geographic exposure, ensuring that the assessment is relevant and tailored. Unlike self-declared ESG disclosures, it relies heavily on verified documentation, including policies, certifications, and performance data. Analysts review the submitted evidence and assign scores that are aggregated into a final rating out of 100. This methodology ensures consistency and comparability, making the score a trusted metric for global procurement teams and stakeholders.

### How long does the EcoVadis assessment process take?

The assessment process typically takes between four to twelve weeks, depending on the company’s preparedness and the complexity of its operations. The timeline includes completing the questionnaire, gathering supporting documents, and undergoing analyst review. Companies with well-established ESG systems and documentation can complete the process more efficiently, while those starting from scratch may require additional time to align their policies and processes. Understanding this timeline is crucial for businesses that need to meet client deadlines or supplier requirements linked to EcoVadis ratings.
