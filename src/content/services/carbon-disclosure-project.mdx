### What is the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP)?

The Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP) is a globally recognized non-profit organization that operates the world’s leading environmental disclosure system. It enables companies, cities, states, and regions to measure and manage their environmental impacts by reporting data related to climate change, water security, and deforestation. Through CDP, organizations can enhance transparency, track progress toward sustainability goals, and build trust with stakeholders by aligning with best practices in environmental responsibility.

### What are the Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) scoring levels (A to D)?

Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) assigns scores based on the quality and completeness of a company’s environmental disclosures. The scoring methodology categorizes responses into four key levels: A (Leadership): Demonstrates best practice in environmental management and strategic integration into business operations. B (Management): Shows awareness of environmental issues and a structured approach to managing them through policies and initiatives. C (Awareness): Reflects recognition of environmental challenges and basic understanding of associated risks and opportunities. D/D- (Disclosure): Indicates transparency in providing environmental data, but without significant environmental governance or action plans. Scoring is done separately for Climate Change, Water Security, and Forests (if applicable), and the goal is to support continuous improvement over time.

### Is Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) reporting mandatory?

No, Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) reporting is not legally required; however, it has become a de facto requirement in many industries due to growing pressure from investors, customers, and regulatory bodies. Voluntary CDP participation can give organizations a competitive advantage by demonstrating commitment to sustainability, improving access to green finance, meeting ESG requirements, and preparing for future regulations. Increasingly, large corporations and financial institutions request their suppliers and partners to disclose through CDP.

### Which companies should report to Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) ?

Any organization with significant environmental impact—especially in sectors like manufacturing, energy, transport, agriculture, and consumer goods—should consider Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) reporting. In particular, companies that are publicly traded, have high greenhouse gas emissions, rely on water-intensive operations, or source from forest-risk commodities are encouraged to participate. Additionally, businesses that are part of Carbon Disclosure project (CDP) Supply Chain programs, where customers request disclosures from suppliers, should respond to maintain their commercial relationships and improve ESG transparency.
