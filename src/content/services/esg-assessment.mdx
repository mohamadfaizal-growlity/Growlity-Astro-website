## What is the S&P ESG Score?

The S&P ESG Score by S&P Global measures a company’s environmental, social, and governance performance using detailed, sector-specific, data-driven analysis. It’s widely used by global investors and across major indices like the S&P 500 to assess ESG risks, opportunities, and maturity.  
  
At Growlity, we help businesses navigate and improve their S&P ESG Scores. From score interpretation to actionable improvement strategies, we support companies in building investor trust, meeting regulatory requirements, and enhancing long-term ESG performance.

### What is the purpose of an ESG assessment?

To evaluate an organization’s current ESG performance, identify gaps, and provide a roadmap for compliance, improvement, and reporting.

### How is ESG materiality determined?

We use stakeholder inputs and sector-specific risks to create a Materiality Matrix, aligned with GRI and CSRD.

### Can Growlity assess ESG risks across supply chains?

Yes, our ESG Supply Chain Assessment covers Tier I–III vendors, evaluates ESG maturity, and provides improvement strategies.

### Do you provide ESG due diligence for investors?

Yes, we offer ESG due diligence support covering environmental risks, regulatory gaps, and social/governance concerns for M&A or funding purposes

### Is this different from ESG reporting?

Yes. ESG assessment is diagnostic; reporting is about communicating those findings. We help with both.

In today’s investor-driven, regulation-focused environment, ESG reporting is no longer optional—it’s a strategic imperative. ESG disclosures influence investment decisions, consumer trust, regulatory compliance, and corporate reputation.
