### What is ESG Reporting and why is it needed?

ESG reporting is the structured disclosure of your company’s Environmental, Social, and Governance performance. It goes beyond traditional financial reporting to reflect how your business manages sustainability-related risks and opportunities. It is essential for improving transparency, meeting regulatory and stakeholder expectations, and building trust with investors, customers, and regulators. A strong ESG report also supports better decision-making, reputation management, and long-term resilience in a rapidly evolving business landscape.

### What ESG framework should I follow?

The right ESG framework depends on your industry, reporting objectives, stakeholder expectations, and regulatory obligations. Companies aiming for comprehensive stakeholder communication often choose the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI), while those targeting investor-specific disclosures may opt for SASB. Climate-related risk reporting aligns well with TCFD, and companies operating in or linked to the EU must consider CSRD requirements. In India, listed companies follow SEBI’s BRSR framework. At Growlity, we help you navigate these options and design a reporting approach that integrates the most relevant standards—ensuring both compliance and impact.

### Is ESG Reporting mandatory in India?

Yes. ESG reporting is mandatory for the top 1000 listed companies in India under SEBI’s Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR) mandate. However, ESG is gaining traction beyond listed companies. SMEs, exporters, and supply chain partners are increasingly adopting ESG voluntarily to meet client requirements, improve credit access, and prepare for global market expectations like CBAM, EU Taxonomy, and investor ratings.

### Can you help with ESG ratings like CDP, MSCI, or EcoVadis?

Absolutely. We provide complete ESG rating support—covering readiness assessment, gap analysis, data collection, evidence mapping, and final submission. Our expertise spans multiple ratings and disclosures including CDP, EcoVadis, MSCI, Sustainalytics, S&P Global, and others. We help you boost scores, close compliance gaps, and enhance your ESG positioning across global supply chains and investor platforms.

### How is ESG different from CSR or SDG Reporting?

While CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) often focuses on voluntary, philanthropic efforts, ESG is performance-based, strategic, and integrated into business operations. SDG (Sustainable Development Goal) reporting aligns your impact with the 17 UN Global Goals, but ESG reporting goes deeper into how environmental and social issues affect your business and vice versa. ESG captures both risk and opportunity, providing a roadmap for long-term value creation and sustainable growth.
