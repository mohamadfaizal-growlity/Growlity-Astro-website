### What is LCA used for?

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) is a powerful tool used to measure and analyze the environmental impacts of a product, service, or process across its entire life cycle—from raw material extraction to end-of-life disposal. It helps organizations identify environmental trade-offs, improve sustainability performance, and make data-driven decisions in product design, supply chain management, and operations. LCA is also used to support regulatory compliance, eco-labeling, Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs), and green marketing initiatives.

### How do ISO 14040 and 14044 relate?

ISO 14040 and ISO 14044 are the international standards that define how Life Cycle Assessments should be conducted. ISO 14040 outlines the overarching principles, framework, and scope of LCA, including goal definition and system boundaries. ISO 14044 provides detailed guidance on methodology, data requirements, impact assessment, interpretation, and reporting. Together, these standards ensure consistency, transparency, and scientific rigor in LCA studies, and are widely used to validate the quality of environmental disclosures.

### Which software is best for LCA?

There are several leading software tools used for conducting LCAs, each with its strengths depending on your project scope and budget: openLCA: A free, open-source tool that is ISO-compliant and widely used for academic, SME, and professional assessments. SimaPro: Known for its robust database access and detailed impact analysis, ideal for product design and corporate reporting. GaBi: Offers comprehensive datasets and is well-suited for industrial applications and complex supply chains. Umberto: Focuses on material and energy flow modeling, offering powerful visualization and data interpretation tools. The best choice depends on your technical needs, available data, and the level of support required.

### Do I need LCA for CDP/BRSR/EPD?

While not always mandatory, having a formal Life Cycle Assessment significantly strengthens your environmental disclosures. For CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project), LCA helps in accurately reporting product-level Scope 3 emissions and environmental risks. For BRSR (Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting in India), LCA supports claims of circularity, eco-design, and resource efficiency. For EPD (Environmental Product Declarations), LCA is a core requirement for verifying and publishing third-party certified environmental impact statements. Using LCA adds transparency, credibility, and alignment with globally accepted frameworks.

### How long does it take to complete an LCA?

The duration of an LCA project can vary depending on the complexity of the product or system being analyzed, the availability of data, and the desired depth of assessment. A basic screening-level LCA may take 2–4 weeks, while a detailed, ISO-compliant study for EPD or third-party disclosure can take 2–3 months. Timelines also depend on the number of life cycle stages covered, the quality of data, and the need for verification. Starting early with proper data organization can greatly streamline the process.
