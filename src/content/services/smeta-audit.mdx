### What is the difference between SMETA and SEDEX?

SEDEX is a platform for sharing supplier information, while SMETA is the audit methodology used to assess ethical compliance.

### How long does a SMETA Audit take?

The duration depends on company size, workforce, and operational complexity.

### Who needs a SMETA Audit?

Manufacturers, exporters, suppliers, and organizations working with global brands often require a SMETA Audit.

### What is covered in a SMETA 4-Pillar Audit?

Labor Standards, Health & Safety, Environment, and Business Ethics.

### How can we prepare for a SMETA Audit?

A gap assessment, documentation review, employee training, and corrective action implementation significantly improve readiness.

### Is SMETA Audit mandatory?

It is not legally mandatory but is frequently required by customers and procurement teams.

### How often should a SMETA Audit be conducted?

Most organizations undergo audits based on customer requirements, typically every 1–3 years.

Yes. We provide end-to-end support for corrective action implementation and continuous improvement.
