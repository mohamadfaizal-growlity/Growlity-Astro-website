### What is the S&P ESG Score used for?

It helps investors, regulators, and businesses evaluate ESG performance across standardized criteria. It’s used in portfolio decisions, risk assessments, and compliance reporting.

### How is the S&P ESG Score different from ratings like EcoVadis or MSCI?

While EcoVadis is used in supply chain assessments, S&P focuses on investor-grade scoring using sector-specific CSA and covers listed and large-scale companies.

### Is it only relevant for S&P 500 companies?

No. While S&P 500 index companies are widely rated, the CSA covers global companies across industries, both listed and private.

### What are the benefits of a high S&P ESG Score?

Better visibility in financial markets, increased trust with stakeholders, improved access to capital, and stronger positioning in ESG-aligned partnerships.
