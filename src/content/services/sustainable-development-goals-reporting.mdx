### What is SDG Reporting and why is it important?

SDG Reporting refers to the disclosure of a company’s impact, risks, and contributions toward the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals. It enhances transparency, builds stakeholder trust, and supports ESG strategy.

### How do we align our company with the UN SDGs?

We start by mapping your business activities to the most relevant SDGs and targets. Then, we help integrate them into your core business and report your progress effectively.

### Is SDG Reporting mandatory?

While not legally mandatory in all countries, it is increasingly expected under frameworks like GRI, BRSR, EU CSRD, and UNGC. It is also key for ESG ratings and sustainable finance.

### Can we include SDGs in our ESG report?

Yes. In fact, we recommend embedding SDG disclosures into your ESG or sustainability report for a holistic view. We help align your KPIs with SDG indicators.

### How does SDG Reporting support climate action or net-zero goals?

SDG 13 (Climate Action) directly links to emission reductions, renewable energy adoption, and resilience planning. SDG Reporting enables you to track and communicate your progress credibly.

In today’s investor-driven, regulation-focused environment, ESG reporting is no longer optional—it’s a strategic imperative. ESG disclosures influence investment decisions, consumer trust, regulatory compliance, and corporate reputation.
