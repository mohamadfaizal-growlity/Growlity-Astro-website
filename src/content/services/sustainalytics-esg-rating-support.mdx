### What is a good Sustainalytics ESG score?

A score below 20 is considered strong (Low or Negligible Risk). The lower the score, the better your ESG risk management appears.

### How is the Sustainalytics risk rating different from ESG ratings like MSCI or S&P?

Sustainalytics focuses on ESG risk—not just policies or disclosures. It is risk-based, while others may focus more on ESG opportunities or commitments.

### Can I improve my Sustainalytics score?

Yes. With the right ESG policies, disclosures, and internal systems, we can help reduce your unmanaged risk score.

### Does Sustainalytics require company-submitted data?

Yes. While Sustainalytics also uses publicly available data, proactive engagement and structured submissions improve score accuracy.

### Is Sustainalytics recognized by investors?

Absolutely. Sustainalytics ratings are widely used by asset managers, ESG funds, and institutional investors globally.
