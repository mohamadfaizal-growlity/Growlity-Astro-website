**About the Webinar  
**The European Union’s **Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)** is reshaping the way global manufacturers engage with the EU market. As the EU tightens its climate policies to prevent carbon leakage, exporters will now be required to measure, report, and eventually pay for the carbon emissions embedded in their products.

For manufacturers exporting to the EU, CBAM is no longer a future concern — it is a **strategic and operational reality**. Non-compliance can result in trade disruptions, penalties, increased costs, and loss of competitiveness in one of the world’s most regulated markets.

To help organizations navigate this complex regulatory landscape, **Growlity** is hosting an in-depth global webinar titled:

**“****Simplifying Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism Reporting (CBAM) : Fundamentals and Key Updates”**

This practical session is designed to demystify CBAM requirements and provide clear guidance on how manufacturers can prepare for compliance, strengthen carbon reporting systems, and align with EU expectations well ahead of enforcement deadlines.

**Session Overview  
**This webinar will provide a comprehensive breakdown of the **CBAM framework**, covering its scope, implementation phases, reporting obligations, and sector-specific impacts. Participants will gain clarity on how CBAM applies to different product categories, what data must be reported, and how carbon emissions are calculated under EU guidelines.

The session will also explore how CBAM aligns with broader climate and trade regulations, and how organizations can integrate CBAM readiness into their existing ESG, GHG accounting, and sustainability reporting processes.

Through real-world insights and practical examples, Growlity’s experts will guide participants on avoiding common compliance pitfalls and preparing robust systems to meet CBAM requirements efficiently and cost-effectively.

**What You’ll Learn in This Webinar**

*   Fundamentals of CBAM
*   CBAM Scope & Coverage
*   CBAM Timelines & Implementation Phases
*   Carbon Reporting & Data Requirements
*   Sector-Specific Implications
*   Link Between CBAM, GHG Accounting & ESG Reporting
*   Preparing Your Organization for CBAM
*   Common Challenges & How to Avoid Them

**Who Should Attend**

*   Manufacturers exporting or planning to export to the EU
*   Sustainability, ESG, and climate professionals
*   Compliance, regulatory, and trade teams
*   Supply chain and operations leaders
*   Finance and risk management professionals
